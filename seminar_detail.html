Therapy Ball Techniques for Early Childhood Skill Development

Sunday April 24, 2013
Registration: 8:30 AM — 9:00 AM
Seminar: 9:00 AM — 4:30 PM

Information

With pressure to cut costs and avoid nursing home stays, the provision of rehabilitation services in the home is growing in leaps and bounds. Home care has become a viable practice area for therapists both on a full time and part time basis.

All therapists working in the homecare arena need to be familiar with the OASIS data tool. The OASIS (Outcome and Assessment Information Set) is a tool which assesses multiple aspects of the patient’s health care status and environment including medical, nursing, rehabilitation, and social issues as well as discharge needs. The form must be completed at various points of the home care coverage period including discharge. The results of the assessment determines the need for homecare services and identifies whether the patient is eligible for the home health benefit under Medicare A.

While typically considered a nursing tool, therapists are expected to have familiarity with the OASIS and be able to independently complete it if necessary. This two hour evening course will review all of the OASIS items and demonstrate proper form completion. By the end of the course participants will be able to demonstrate competency in the completion of the OASIS.

Who Should Attend
Occupational Therapists, Physical Therapists, Speech Language Pathologists

Learning Outcomes
Participants will:
Identify the importance of the OASIS document.
Discern between the different parts of the OASIS.
Be able to complete an OASIS document.
